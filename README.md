# NINJ1-gingival-aging
Scripts and analysis workflows for single-cell RNA-seq and multi-omics integration analyses investigating macrophage NINJ1-mediated plasma membrane rupture and senescence in the aged gingival microenvironment.

# Article
Activation of Macrophage NINJ1 Drives Gingival Aging

# Abstract
Aging alters tissue microenvironments to promote chronic inflammation, prominently manifesting as periodontal disease. However, the early molecular triggers within the aging gingival microenvironment remain poorly defined. Through single-cell RNA sequencing on gingival tissues from young and aged mice, we identified Ninj1, a key executioner of plasma membrane rupture, as a significantly up-regulated target in the aged gingiva. Further cell-type annotation revealed that Ninj1 upregulation was most prominent in macrophages, and this finding was further validated in human datasets. Functionally, overexpressed NINJ1 accumulated at the plasma membrane and triggered lytic membrane rupture. Supernatants from ruptured macrophages induced senescence in epithelial cells, endothelial cells, and periodontal ligament stem cells (PDLSCs), as evidenced by increased SA-β-gal activity and γH2AX foci. Proteomic and metabolomic profiling of the macrophage secretome identified critical rupture-associated components, such as CXCL10 and xanthine. Crucially, these factors drove cellular senescence and suppressed the osteogenic differentiation of PDLSCs. Together, our findings identify the activation of macrophage NINJ1 as a driver of periodontal tissue senescence, offering a potential therapeutic target for age-related periodontal disease.
